# Standing Requirements

## 1. Introduction
This document outlines the standing requirements for wards/branches and municipalities in the membership system. These requirements ensure that organizational units maintain adequate membership levels and representation.

## 2. Ward/Branch Standing Requirements

### 2.1 Good Standing Criteria
For a ward/branch to be considered in **good standing**, it must meet the following criteria:

- **Minimum Membership**: The ward/branch must have **200 or more active members**.
- **Active Status**: The ward/branch must be officially recognized in the system.

### 2.2 Benefits of Good Standing
Wards/branches in good standing receive the following benefits:

- Eligibility to participate in regional and national events
- Voting rights in municipal-level decisions
- Access to organizational resources and support
- Visual indicators in the system showing good standing status

### 2.3 Consequences of Not Being in Good Standing
Wards/branches not in good standing:

- May have limited participation in certain organizational activities
- Will be flagged in the system for membership development focus
- Will receive additional support to increase membership

## 3. Municipality Representation Requirements

### 3.1 Representation Criteria
For a municipality to be considered adequately represented, it must meet the following criteria:

- **Minimum Ward Representation**: At least **65% of the wards** within the municipality must be in **good standing** (have 200+ members).

This representation percentage is calculated as: (Number of wards with 200+ members / Total number of wards in the municipality) Ã— 100%.

### 3.2 Benefits of Adequate Representation
Municipalities with adequate representation receive the following benefits:

- Eligibility to participate in provincial and national decision-making
- Priority access to organizational resources
- Recognition in organizational reports and communications

### 3.3 Consequences of Inadequate Representation
Municipalities without adequate representation:

- May have limited influence in regional and national decisions
- Will be prioritized for membership development initiatives
- Will receive additional support to increase ward-level membership

## 4. Implementation

### 4.1 System Features
The membership system will implement these requirements through:

- Automatic calculation of ward standing based on member count
- Automatic calculation of municipality representation percentage
- Visual indicators in the UI for standing status
- Filtering and sorting options based on standing status
- Reports and analytics focused on standing and representation metrics

### 4.2 Monitoring and Reporting
- Standing status will be updated in real-time as members are added or removed
- Regular reports will be generated showing standing status across the organization
- Trend analysis will track improvements in standing over time

## 5. Transition Plan

### 5.1 Implementation Timeline
- All existing wards and municipalities will be evaluated against the new standing criteria
- A grace period of 3 months will be provided for wards to reach the minimum membership requirement
- Support resources will be allocated to help wards achieve good standing status

### 5.2 Communication Plan
- All organizational levels will be informed of the new standing requirements
- Training will be provided on how to interpret standing indicators in the system
- Regular updates will be sent on progress toward meeting standing requirements
